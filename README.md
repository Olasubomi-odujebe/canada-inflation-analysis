# Canada's Inflation Rate (2015–2026): Has Price Stability Returned?

## Overview

This project analyzes Canada's monthly inflation trends using the Consumer Price Index (CPI) from Statistics Canada. The analysis examines how inflation evolved between **2015 and 2026**, calculates the year-over-year inflation rate, and compares it with the **Bank of Canada's 2% inflation target** to assess whether price stability has been restored.

The project was developed using **R** and **Quarto** as part of my macroeconomic analysis portfolio.

---

## Research Question

**Has price stability returned to Canada following the post-pandemic inflation surge?**

---

## Objectives

- Examine the long-term trend in Canada's Consumer Price Index (CPI).
- Calculate Canada's year-over-year inflation rate.
- Compare inflation with the Bank of Canada's 2% inflation target.
- Discuss the implications of inflation for the Canadian economy.

---

## Data Source

- **Source:** Statistics Canada
- **Dataset:** Consumer Price Index, Monthly, Not Seasonally Adjusted (Table 18-10-0004-01)
- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Coverage:** Canada
- **Period:** January 2015 – May 2026

## Key Findings

- The average inflation rate between 2016 and 2026 was **2.65%**.
- Inflation peaked at **8.13%** in 2022.
- Inflation briefly turned negative during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Although inflation has moderated considerably, it remains slightly above the Bank of Canada's 2% target.
